📊 Schedule Counter

Cockrell Hill — Staffing Analysis & Labor Optimization

Version 3.1 · Built for non-technical operators

What Is This?

Schedule Counter combines your HotSchedules export with daily sales data to show exactly where you’re overstaffed, understaffed, or on target — hour by hour, day by day. It replaces the guesswork of “do we have enough people?” with a color-coded grid, actionable insights, and automated weekly forecasts. Everything runs inside Google Sheets with no external services required.

Tab-by-Tab Guide

1 File Converter

Converts your raw HotSchedules “Schedule Report” export (Excel/HTML format) into a clean hourly CSV that Schedule Analysis can read. Upload the file, click Convert & Download, and save the CSV. You need to pull FOH and BOH separately from HotSchedules to get per-department analysis.

How to get the file: HotSchedules → Reporting → Dashboard → Schedules and Rosters → Schedule Report. Set output to Excel XP–2007. Check “Include Out Times” and “Total Day” under Available Shifts.

2 Schedule Analysis

The main analysis screen. Upload your FOH and/or BOH CSVs from Step 1, enter daily sales estimates (or let the forecast fill them), and click Generate Analysis. You get:

  • Color-coded staffing grid — green = on target, red = understaffed, blue overlay = sales intensity
  • Metrics dashboard — avg staff/hour, overall $/LH (SPLH), total labor hours, overtime detection
  • Department views — toggle between Overall, FOH, and BOH
  • Daypart filters — breakfast, lunch, afternoon, dinner, late night
  • Optimization insights — specific recommendations on where to add or cut staff
  • Ideal staffing model — shows exactly how many people you need per hour to hit your SPLH goal
  • Manager shift view — highlights manager coverage gaps
Tip: Every time you generate, the data is saved to history automatically. You can re-upload different versions freely — no emails are sent until Thursday.

📈 Trends

Week-over-week performance tracking. Shows SPLH trends, goal distribution over time, weekly total sales history, and a drill-down for any individual week. Also includes forecast accuracy flags that detect when your sales curves are consistently over- or under-estimating for specific days. Data populates automatically as you upload schedules and as the Monday pipeline archives real CFA sales.

⚙️ Settings

All configuration lives here. Nothing is hardcoded — operators can adjust everything:

  • SPLH Goals — set overall, FOH, and BOH targets (default $90/LH)
  • Labor Shares — how sales are split between FOH/BOH (e.g., 50/40/10)
  • Sales Curves — per-day hourly weight distributions (auto-updated by the pipeline, manually editable)
  • Location & Notifications — location name and operator email for reminders
  • Advanced — smoothing alpha and bias threshold (rarely need changing)
  • Automation — install/check triggers, test pipeline, backfill history, rebuild curves, test reminder

📈 Productivity Tracker

A standalone monthly productivity view. Upload or enter monthly SPLH figures to track long-term trends and compare before/after periods (e.g., before vs. after implementing schedule changes). Includes a trend chart and statistical breakdown.

🔮 Forecast

Predicts daily total sales for the week you’re scheduling (2 weeks out). Uses a linearly-weighted moving average with outlier exclusion and trend adjustment across your last 8 weeks of CFA sales data. Each day shows a predicted total, trend direction, and how many weeks of data were used. More history = more accurate forecasts.

How Automation Works

Three time-driven triggers run automatically each week. Install them once from Settings → Automation.

🔻
Sunday 10:00 PM — Sync Check Validates that CFA sales data arrived in Sheet1. If the sync from CFA didn’t run or Sheet1 is empty, you get an alert email so you can fix it before Monday.
▶️
Monday 6:00 AM — Weekly Pipeline The main automation. Reads Sheet1, archives sales to history, runs exponential smoothing to update your sales curves, detects forecast bias, cleans up old data (>90 days), clears Sheet1, and rebuilds the app cache. All before your day starts.
📧
Thursday 7:00 PM — Schedule Reminder Sends a reminder email to the operator to post next week’s schedule. Includes a summary of the latest uploaded schedule’s performance (SPLH, goal breakdown, staffing hours, daily sales) so you have context as you build the next one.
How Sales Curves Work

Sales curves control how your daily sales estimate gets distributed across hours. Each day (Monday–Saturday) has its own curve of 19 hourly weights that sum to 1.0.

Default Curve Starts with a generic distribution (peak at 10 AM–12 PM). Applied to all days until real data arrives.
Exponential Smoothing Each Monday, the pipeline blends last week’s actual hourly sales fractions into the curve using α=0.3. New data gets 30% weight, existing curve keeps 70%. Curves adapt gradually.
Outlier Protection Before smoothing, hourly fractions are capped at mean + 2 standard deviations of the current curve. This prevents one large catering order from distorting the entire day’s curve.
Manual Override You can always edit any day’s curve manually in Settings → Sales Curves. Weights must sum to 1.000. The pipeline will continue smoothing from your manual values going forward.
How the Forecast Works

The Forecast tab predicts total sales for each day of the week you’re scheduling (2 weeks out).

  1. Window: Takes the last N weeks of daily totals from sales_history (default 8 weeks, configurable via forecast_weeks in the config sheet).
  2. Outlier exclusion: Removes values more than 2 standard deviations from the mean (with at least 4 weeks of data). Never drops below 3 data points.
  3. Linearly-weighted average: Recent weeks count more. Week 8 gets 8x the weight of week 1.
  4. Trend adjustment: Compares the recent half to the older half. If sales are trending up or down, adds a per-week slope adjustment, capped at ±10% of the average.
Data Architecture

All data lives in Google Sheets tabs. No external databases or services.

Sheet Tab Purpose Written By

sales_history Hourly sales per day per week (estimated + actual/api) Report generation / Monday pipeline
sales_curves Per-day hourly weight distributions (19 hours × 6 days) Monday pipeline / manual edit

Key Concepts
SPLH (Sales Per Labor Hour) The core metric. Total sales for an hour ÷ number of staff working that hour. Higher = more productive. Your goal (default $90) sets the target.
Labor Shares How total sales are attributed to FOH vs. BOH. If FOH is 50% and BOH is 40%, then FOH gets credit for 50/(50+40) = 55.6% of sales when computing department SPLH.
Bias Detection Compares your estimated hourly sales (from the curve) to actual CFA sales for each day. If a day is consistently over- or under-estimated beyond the threshold (default 10%), a bias flag appears on the analysis screen.
Idempotent Design Everything is safe to re-run. Uploading the same week twice overwrites the previous data. Running the pipeline twice won’t duplicate rows. Setup functions skip tabs that already exist.
Typical Weekly Workflow
Monday AM
Pipeline runs automatically at 6 AM. Archives last week’s CFA sales, updates curves, rebuilds cache.
Thursday PM
Reminder email arrives at 7 PM with last schedule’s performance summary. Open the app, check the Forecast tab for next week’s predicted sales.
Thu/Fri
Build the schedule in HotSchedules. Export it, convert with File Converter, upload to Schedule Analysis with forecast sales numbers. Iterate until the grid looks good.
Friday
Post the final schedule. The last version you uploaded is saved to history for trend tracking.
Sunday PM
Sync check runs at 10 PM. Verifies CFA data landed in Sheet1 for Monday’s pipeline. Alerts you if something’s missing.
Troubleshooting
Forecast shows “Not enough sales history” The Monday pipeline hasn’t run yet, or Sheet1 never had data. Go to Settings → Automation → Backfill History to import any existing data, then Rebuild Curves.
SPLH shows $0 or metrics look wrong Make sure you entered daily sales estimates in the sales bar on the Schedule Analysis tab. Without sales data, SPLH can’t be calculated.
Optimization insights recommend unrealistic staffing This usually means catering orders inflated the sales curve for certain hours. Go to Settings → Automation → Rebuild Curves to reprocess all history with outlier protection applied.
Triggers aren’t firing Go to Settings → Automation and click Refresh Status. If a trigger shows as not installed, click Install Triggers. You can also use Test Pipeline Now or Test Reminder to verify manually.
