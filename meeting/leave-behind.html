Tools that give the team their time back

Brent Baldwin · Chief of Staff · Cockrell Hill Chick-fil-A
brentbaldwin.com

Every tool here started the same way: I wanted to make life easier for my team. Give people back their time, a little privacy, and a say in their own week — and they stop leaving. The operational wins came after, almost as a byproduct.

One pattern under all of it: a trigger feeds a pipeline, a pipeline feeds a cache, a cache feeds a web app, the app emails the right person at the right time — and a non-technical manager keeps it running by editing a spreadsheet.

For the team

Self-service, in English & Spanish — no manager required

Request time off yourself

Put in a PTO request from your phone — pick the dates and submit. No catching a manager between rushes, no waiting on a text back. Your time off, your request.

Order uniforms with the cost up front

Browse the catalog, pick your sizes, and see exactly what each item costs and what will come out of each paycheck before you ever hit submit. No surprises on payday.

Payroll that’s clear

PTO, overtime, and uniform deductions live in one place with the math already done — so people can see what they earned and what was taken out, and trust it.

For the operation

The wins that follow when people’s time is protected

Schedule Counter

Builds the schedule around people’s real lives while holding labor productivity. SPLH $84.42 → $91.02, past goal since Feb 2025.

Payroll System

One place for the whole pay period — including cross-location overtime reconciled correctly across both stores (FLSA). Processing 2–3 hr → 30 min.

Catering Quote Generator & H.E.A.R.D. Log

Catering quotes 10–20 min → 2–3 min; guest recovery logged in under a minute with repeat-complainer alerts. Food cost gap 1.02% → 0.74%.

How — and why — I build them this way

  • I lead before I build. The software is the easy part; the tools exist to free up the team’s time so they can focus on the guest and on each other.
  • Designed for whoever comes after me: idempotent setup, config in the spreadsheet, maintained by a non-technical operator.
  • Security-conscious: I run a custom risk-audit scanner and XSS hardening passes over my own code.
  • Honest about scope: built for two locations today, with a clear view of what would change to run at chain scale.
